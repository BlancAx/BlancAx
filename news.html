The ACT REPEALING FOR THE PURPOSE REPUBLIC ACT NO. 9372 OTHERWISE KNOWN AS THE "HUMAN SECURITY ACT (HSA)
OF 2007" AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR or the Anti- Terrorism Bill was passed by the Senate to "prevent and stop" terrorist attacks in the country by directly targeting individuals who are profiled to be most likely to join considered terrorist groups or perform such acts.

If this bill is passed to be an act, the state and its forces will have the absolute power in defining what constitutes the definition of terrorism, who are to be considered terrorists, and what to do with these individuals. The act itself assumes that the state is the most rational in society, therefore, any suspicion by state institutions such as PNP and AFP is enough bass for being considered terrorists.

The Anti-Terror Bill also allows police, law enforcement, and military personnel duly authorized by the Anti-Terrorism Council (ATC) to carry out warrantless arrests without incurring liability under Article 125 of the Revised Penal Code. Also, the allowable period of detention has been extended to 14 working days, extendible by 10 days.

Cases of abuse using the HSA - like those of Lumad leader Jomorito Goaynon and peasant organizer Ireneo Udarbe - can happen to anyone under this worse bill. They were arrested on January 28, 2019 in Talakag, Bukidnon. They were charged with illegal possession of firearms and explosive, violation of the election gun ban, rebellion, and violations of the HSA.

With this bill, the government is in full position to kill dissent and opposition.
