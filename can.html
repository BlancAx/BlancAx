The Anti-Terror Bill also allows police, law enforcement, and military personnel duly authorized by the Anti-Terrorism Council (ATC) to carry out warrantless arrests without incurring liability under Article 125 of the Revised Penal Code. Also, the allowable period of detention has been extended to 14 working days, extendible by 10 days.

On Constitution Day, Feb 2, the Supreme Court began hearing oral arguments on the 37 petitions filed against the anti-terror law that is seen to endanger rights protected in the Bill of Rights of the 1987 Philippines Constitution.

What are the actions that we can take to contribute towards the junking of the Anti-Terror Bill?


With Its current status, the legislators and senators who support the bill can only be swayed through public pressure and collective action of the people. Student Councils should encourage the student body to join progressive organizations that will help students to be more aware and involved in social issues. Councils as leaders should see that one of their main duty is to ensure that their constituents are always ready to struggle for democracy. Your Council can invite students to also join Congress and Senate lobbying, write letters to your representatives and senators, and initiate signature campaigns.


We know that social media is a powerful tool in combatting fake pieces of information roaming around the internet. Your Council can release informative graphics regarding the details that we should know about the bill and how it will affect the general population. Do note that although we acknowledge the power of social media, it is best for Councils to organize public forums/barangay meetings in communities to help spread the information and educate the individuals on the ground.
